Relevant physical scales

The star we can observe best is the closest one, the Sun (o), so a lot of quantities are scaled to those of the Sun in stellar physics and in astronomy more generally.

  • Solar radius

    Ro = 6.957× 1010 cm ≅ 1011 cm

  • Solar mass

    Mo = 2×1033 g

  • Solar luminosity

    Lo = 3.82×1033nilnil erg s-1 ≅ 2× Mo in cgs units!

    This may be one of the reasons why we still use cgs in astronomy, the other one being that the constants in electromagnetism are a bit simpler.

  • Lifetime

    Lifetimes: ~3 Myr to ≫ age of the Universe (≅ 13.7 Gyr)

    How old is the Sun? How long will it live?
